Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services, process, and how to get started.

Reinvent Strategic Solutions partners with mission-driven organizations, academic institutions, nonprofits, and community-based initiatives. We also work with select private-sector organizations whose work intersects with education, public health, behavioral health, workforce development, or equity-focused initiatives.

Our services include:

  • Research design (qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods)
  • Program evaluation and impact assessment
  • Strategic planning and program development
  • Academic and dissertation support for doctoral students
  • Advisory and consultation services for organizations navigating complex decisions

Each engagement is tailored to the client's context, goals, and capacity.

We work with organizations at all stages—from early program design and pilot development to implementation, evaluation, and outcome assessment. Whether you are refining an existing program or building something new, we adapt our approach to meet you where you are.

An initial consultation is a focused conversation to understand your goals, challenges, and timeline. We'll discuss your needs, explore possible approaches, and determine whether there is a good fit for working together. There is no obligation to move forward after the consultation.

Project length varies depending on scope. Some advisory engagements may span a few weeks, while research and evaluation projects often extend over several months. We clearly define timelines, milestones, and deliverables at the outset of each engagement.

Yes. We offer customized engagement models based on your needs, including short-term advisory support, phased evaluation projects, and longer-term research partnerships. Flexibility is central to our approach.

Equity and cultural responsiveness are foundational to our practice. We design research and evaluation processes that are inclusive, context-aware, and respectful of the communities involved. This includes attention to cultural identity, access, power dynamics, and the practical implications of findings for diverse stakeholders.

Yes. We support doctoral students and practitioner-scholars with research design, methodology selection, proposal development, IRB preparation, data analysis, and structured writing support. Our approach emphasizes clarity, rigor, and feasibility.

Our experience spans multiple sectors, including:

  • Education and higher education
  • Behavioral and public health
  • Commmunity based organizations and youth development
  • Workforce development and training initiatives
  • Organizational operations and leadership

We draw from interdisciplinary research traditions to support complex, real-world problems.

We integrate principles of Cultural Intelligence (CQ) into consulting, research, and evaluation efforts to support organizations operating in culturally diverse environments. This includes applying culturally informed frameworks to decision-making, learning, and evaluation practices.
